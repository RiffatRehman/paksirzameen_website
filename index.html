About , Pakistan

Pakistan, a land of rich history and vibrant culture, is known for its breathtaking landscapes ranging from majestic mountains to serene beaches. Founded in 1947 as a homeland for Muslims of the Indian subcontinent, Pakistan embodies the spirit of resilience, diversity, and unity. The country boasts a deep heritage with ancient civilizations like Mohenjo-Daro and Harappa, alongside modern cities bustling with progress. Pakistan is famous for its warm hospitality, flavorful cuisine, and passionate people who strive for peace and prosperity. With its strategic location, dynamic economy, and youthful population, Pakistan continues to grow and shine on the global stage.

National Heroes of Pakistan

Allama Iqbal

Allama Iqbal, Pakistan’s national poet, inspired the idea of an independent Muslim state through his poetry and philosophy. He is remembered as the spiritual father of Pakistan, promoting self-awareness and unity among Muslims.

Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah

Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah was the founder of Pakistan and its first Governor-General. He led the country’s independence movement with vision and determination, earning the title “Father of the Nation.”

Last updated 3 mins ago
Fatima Jinnah

Fatima Jinnah, known as the "Mother of the Nation," was a leading figure in Pakistan’s independence movement. She tirelessly supported Quaid-e-Azam Muhammad Ali Jinnah and advocated for women's rights and social justice.

Last updated 3 mins ago
Orangzaib Ahmed

Orangzaib Ahmed played a key role in Operation Bunyan-ul-Marsus (May 2025), showcasing bravery and leadership. His efforts contributed significantly to the success of this critical military operation for Pakistan.

Last updated 3 mins ago
Abdul Qadeer Khan

Abdul Qadeer Khan, revered as the father of Pakistan's nuclear bomb, has died at 85, state-run broadcaster PTV reported on October 10, 2021 [File: Aamir Qureshi/AFP]

Abdul Sattar Edhi

Abdul Sattar Edhi (1928–2016) was a legendary Pakistani philanthropist and founder of the Edhi Foundation. He devoted his life to helping the poor, providing free ambulances, shelters, and healthcare across Pakistan. Known as the "Angel of Mercy," his humanitarian work touched millions and continues to inspire..

Historic Milestones of Pakistan

Independence Day

14 August 1947 – Pakistan emerged as an independent nation after years of struggle.

Lahore Resolution

23 March 1940 – A vision for a separate homeland was formally presented.

Nuclear Power

28 May 1998 – Pakistan became the first nuclear power in the Muslim world.

1965 War Victory

Pakistan defended its sovereignty with courage and unity.

Operation Al-Masoos

May 2025 – Successful defense operation securing national safety.

Humanitarian Strength

From Edhi’s compassion to community aid, Pakistan thrives in unity.

Constitution of 1973

Ensured a democratic framework and civil rights for the people.

Sports Triumph

1992 – Pakistan won the Cricket World Cup, uniting the nation in pride.
